"How I Met Your Mother" is a popular American sitcom created by Craig Thomas and Carter Bays for
CBS, which aired from September 2005 to March 2014. The show follows the life of Ted Mosby and his
friends in Manhattan. The series is a joint production by Bays & Thomas Productions and 20th Century
Fox Television and syndicated by 20th Television (now Disney-ABC Domestic Television).

The show's unique structure, humor, and dramatic elements made it popular throughout its run. Pamela
Fryman directed the vast majority of episodes, with other directors including Rob Greenberg, Michael
Shea, and Neil Patrick Harris. The show was nominated for 91 awards and received 21, including the
People's Choice Award for Favorite Network TV Comedy in 2012.
